Guide: FF1C Request

This page is a summary of how to request to attend the FF1C and all the required materials you must tackle before you attend. The process is subject to change so read any new instructions you receive carefully.

Basic Strategy

Request to attend as you are. Don't fret over all the required docs all at once.

  1. Submit the online request form now to be considered internally.
  2. Be proactive about obtaining all your required materials (online certificates, DMV docs, financial paperwork).
  3. Once you're green-lit internally, we can button up loose ends (Letter of Rec from CO) and you should be good to go for a concrete FF1C start date.

1. Submit Request Form

The online FF1C request form is geared towards Army / Air National Guardsmen. There's stuff that doesn't apply to us (DoD ID, MOS, etc). Either ask what to put or write in "Not Applicable" to get thru the form.

Submitting this now will get your name out there. I'd resubmit this every few months because the Task Force tries to run FF1Cs quarterly.

From the JTFR Job Landing Page fill out this form for to request to attend the FF1C

2. Prerequisite Online Courses

The required online courses were developed by the National Fire Academy (NFA) and National Wildfire Coordinating Group (NWCG)

You have to register in two places to access the courses.

Register with FEMA

Obtain FEMA Student Identification Number (SID):

  • Register for FEMA SID here.
  • Log in with your SID here to check that it works.
  • Take note of your SID

Register with NFA Online

Obtain a FEMA Online User ID (UID):

  • Register for NFA Online here
    • Scroll down and fill out the registration form (requires your SID)
      • Security Domain = USFA
      • Leave "Organizational Information" blank
      • Submit
  • Retrieve NFA Online User ID from your email
    • Wait an hour before taking a course

Take the courses

Find Courses

  1. Log onto FEMA Online here with your UID (not SID )
  2. Click "Home" > "Learning"
  3. Use the search bar to find courses

Required FEMA Online courses (5)

  • S-190: Introduction to Wildland Fire Behavior
  • L-180: Human Factors in the Wildland Fire Service
  • S-130: Firefighting Training
  • ICS-100: Introduction to the Incident Command System
  • ICS-200: Basic Incident Command System for Initial Response

5. CSG Leadership Approval

  • Formally, you need a Letter of Recommendation from our unit's Commanding Officer
    • We can request this once you are internally accepted to attend the FF1C
    • Use your Chain of Command
  • Informally, you need approval from Chief Webb to be accepted to attend the FF1C
    • Or to put it another way, he has the power to veto your attendance.

How do I get approval from Chief Webb?

At drill if Chief Webb is leading an FTX or PT test, give your best effort. Demonstrate that you are physically capable. Demonstrate that you are a team-player and are able to look out for yourself and others. Introduce yourself to him and express your interest in becoming a firefighter.
