Purpose

In order to be absent from drill, you must obtain an approved Form 1003.

The purpose of a Form 1003 is to:

  • document the drill absence and reason
  • determine the duty to be performed in lieu of drill
  • certify that said duty was performed

If you are absent for two consecutive drills, you must attend the next drill.

The basic idea is that you cannot simply skip drill. You must perform an approved substitute duty (ie a SUTA: Substitute Unit Training Assembly).

Form 1003 Approval

  • Via your Chain of Command the Platoon Leader (PL) will receive and either approve or deny your Form 1003 (box 8a.)
    • In the case of a personal schedule conflict, this is when the PL will tack on a training assignment for you to complete as your substitute duty.
  • Next the Commanding Officer (CO) will either authorize or reject the PL's approval.

Form 1003 Certification

  • Once you have completed the substitute duty, your Form 1003 can be certified (Section III), likely by the Commanding Officer (CO).
  • Certification requires evidence that the substitute duty was completed. Examples of evidence include a course certificate or a PERSTAT (if you were on paid orders).

Disclaimer

To date (MAR2026) I have never gotten an approved / authorized / certified Form 1003 back. The above is my understanding of how the Form 1003 approval & certification process is supposed to work.

Typically "no news is good news" after submitting a Form 1003. When there's an issue it will get kicked back to you quickly.

If you are adamant that you need a certified Form 1003 (for example to claim scholarship money), your ASL / SL can help you pursue that. A certified Form 1003 is your proof that you were absent from drill in a legitimate way.
