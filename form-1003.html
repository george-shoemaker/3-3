Form 1003 (JAN2025)

In order to be absent from drill, you must obtain an approved Form 1003. Otherwise you will be considered Absent Without Leave (AWOL üò†).

If you are absent for two consecutive drills, you must attend the next drill.

The purpose of a Form 1003 is to:

  • document the drill absence and reason
  • determine the duty to be performed in lieu of drill
  • certify that said duty was performed

The basic idea is that you cannot simply skip drill. You must perform an approved substitute duty.

Personal Event Conflict

Have a wedding to go to? Does it conflict with drill? Check theüîí2025-2026 YTC : PDF and submit a Form 1003 ASAP!

For a personal event conflict, expect to be assigned substitute training such as online FEMA courses.

Submission more than 30 days out is the expectation. Last-minute Form 1003s will be heavily scrutinized. Only legitimate emergencies will be considered for approval under 2 weeks before drill.

CSG Assignment as Substitute Duty

If there is a CSG assignment that conflicts with drill (such as BTC, 1C, a deployment) select "‚òí PRESENT AT ALTERNATE LOCATION" on your Form 1003 and submit for approval.

If there is a CSG assignment that does not conflict with drill for the month you can optionally submit a Form 1003 to request drill absence. Mark "‚òí SUTA BEFORE THE DRILL" on your Form 1003 and submit for approval.

Form 1003 Approval

  • Via your Chain of Command the Platoon Leader (PL) will receive and either approve or deny your Form 1003 (box 8a.)
  • In the case of a personal schedule conflict, this is when the PL will tack on a training assignment for you to complete as your substitute duty.

Form 1003 Certification

  • Once you have completed the substitute duty, your Form 1003 can be certified (Section III), likely by the Commanding Officer (CO).
  • Certification requires evidence that the substitute duty was completed. Examples of evidence include a course certificate or a PERSTAT (if you were on paid orders).

Disclaimer

To date (OCT2025) I have never gotten an approved or certified Form 1003 back. The above is my understanding of how the Form 1003 approval & certification process is supposed to work.

Typically "no news is good news" after submitting a Form 1003. When there's an issue it will get kicked back to you quickly.

If you are adamant that you need a certified Form 1003 (for example to claim scholarship money), your ASL / SL can help you pursue that. A certified Form 1003 is your proof that you were absent from drill in a legitimate way.
